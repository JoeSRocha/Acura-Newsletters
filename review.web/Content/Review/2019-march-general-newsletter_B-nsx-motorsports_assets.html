BREAKING NEW GROUND
After a thrilling 2018 racing season that featured 14 podium finishes and 3 team wins, Acura Motorsports is shifting into even higher gear this year. Perhaps the most notable team to watch in 2019 is the Acura Team Meyer Shank in the #57 Acura NSX GT3 Evo.
Sponsored by Caterpillar, this all-female race team includes five incredibly talented drivers from across the globe: Christina Nielsen of Denmark, Katherine Legge of England, Simona De Silvestro of Switzerland, Bia Figueiredo of Brazil, and Jackie Heinricher of the U.S.

A STAR IS BORN
2019 marks the 30th anniversary of the shot heard ‘round the automotive world – the global debut of the first-generation Acura NSX. Introduced in concept form at the 1989 Chicago Auto Show, the NSX earned instant praise with its sleek styling and all-aluminum construction.
But it was our supercar’s combination of power, comfort and daily drivability that took the wold by storm and helped the NSX become a beloved automotive icon.

DID YOU KNOW?
NSX stands for “New”, “Sportscar” “eXperimental.”
Our groundbreaking supercar became the world's first mass-produced vehicle with an all-aluminum body when it launched in 1990.

SH-AWD® TURNS 15
Time flies when you’re having fun driving!
Believe it or not, Super Handling All-Wheel Drive™ turns 15 this year. Our groundbreaking SH-AWD® technology transfers power not only between the front and rear wheels, but also between the left and right.
With early roots in the MDX, it’s long set the bar for AWD systems and is now available on five of six Acura models. Ready to see for yourself why SH-AWD® is so incredible?
