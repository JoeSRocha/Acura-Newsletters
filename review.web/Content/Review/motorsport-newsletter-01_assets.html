The American racing season is underway and Acura Motorsports is primed to build on the success of previous campaigns. This season Team Penske will compete in the Prototype class of the IMSA WeatherTech SportsCar Championship with the Acura ARX-05.
Michael Shank Racing will return to field the NSX GT3 in the GT Daytona class. Acura is also teaming up again with RealTime Racing™ for the SCCA Pirelli World Challenge, where the NSX GT3 will contend in the supercar segment.

The green flag has finally dropped on the 2018 The IMSA WeatherTech SportsCar Championship and Acura is already making moves.
Competing in two separate classes at the season-opening Rolex 24 in Daytona, Acura Motorsports contended for victory in both the headline Prototype and supporting GTD divisions.

MEET THE ARX-05
The ARX-05 [Acura Racing eXperimental, generation 5] is the latest in a line of endurance prototypes Acura has fielded since 1991. This awesome machine features Acura-specific bodywork and utilizes the race-proven, production-based Acura AR35TT twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter V6 engine that’s powered numerous class winners.
The ARX-05 will be piloted this season by a dream team lineup including winning INDYCAR racer Helio Castroneves, recently-crowned IMSA Prototype Champion Ricky Taylor, two-time Indianapolis 500 winner Juan Pablo Montoya, and reigning IMSA WeatherTech SportsCar series champion Dane Cameron.
