The 2019 NSX is available to order with new show-stopping color options.
 
From the first 24 hours of racing at Daytona to the final exciting seconds at Road Atlanta, Acura Motorsports never let off the gas in 2018. This season was certainly one to remember, with 14 podium finishes, 3 team wins, and championship fights that went down to the wire. Record-setting performances were even delivered “in the clouds” as well.
 
 
Acura Motorsports competed in two class divisions this season, GT Daytona (GTD) and Prototype. Meyer Shank Racing earned two GTD course victories in the #86 NSX GT3 (at Belle Isle and Laguna Seca) and ended the season ranked 2nd overall. Acura Team Penske took the checkered flag in the ARX-05 Prototype at Belle Isle and finished the season in the 5th place spot. Acura also contended for both the Prototype and GTD manufacturers’ crowns in the Tequila Patron North American Endurance Championship, finishing second in Prototype and third in GTD.
While outstanding performances abounded in 2018, Katherine Legge was the clear standout this season. As lead driver of the #86 NSX GT3, she piloted the car to a second place overall team finish. She also contended for individual points leader, finishing just four points shy of first place. Her 329 overall points bested 105 other drivers.
Acura Motorsports impressed off the track as well, setting three course records at the 2018 Pikes Peak International Hill Climb, a.k.a “Race to the Clouds.” NSX earned a new hybrid record; TLX GT won the Open Class with a record-setting run; and TLX A-Spec® set a new FWD record. The 2019 Acura RDX also scored a podium finish in its race debut.

Although the roaring crowds have now gone silent, a new racing season is right around the corner. Acura Motorsports is looking forward to contending for championships in 2019, and new racing teams are currently being assembled.
Heinricher Racing has already announced a partnership with Meyer Shank Racing and Caterpillar to compete in the 2019 WeatherTech SportsCar Championship in the #57 Acura NSX GT3. This groundbreaking, all-female race team will include four incredible drivers: Jackie Heinricher of the U.S., Katherine Legge of England, Simona De Silvestro of Switzerland, and Bia Figueiredo (Ana Beatriz) of Brazil.
Also returning stronger than ever in 2019 is the Acura NSX GT3 Customer Racing Program. Discerning racing teams will enjoy the latest evolution of the multiple race-winning Acura NSX GT3, designated the NSX GT3 Evo. Eligible to compete on racetracks throughout the world, the NSX GT3 Evo features significant upgrades including new drag-reducing bodywork and new turbochargers for improved throttle response and fuel economy.
