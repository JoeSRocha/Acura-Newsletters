DETERMINED IN DAYTONA
The 2019 IMSA WeatherTech SportsCar Championship is officially underway and Acura Motorsports is off to a fast start. At the season-opening Rolex 24 in Daytona, Acura Team Penske earned a hard-fought podium finish in the #7 ARX-05 under heavy rain and low visibility. Meyer Shank Racing also scored a fifth-place result in the #86 NSX GT3 Evo.
Next up is central Florida (and hopefully some sunshine) for the 12 Hours of Sebring on Saturday, March 16th.

From the first 24 hours of racing at Daytona to the final thrilling seconds at Road Atlanta, Acura Motorsports never let off the gas in 2018. Last year's racing season was certainly one to remember, with 14 podium finishes, 3 team wins, and a championship fight that went down to the wire. Record-setting performances were even delivered “in the clouds“ as well.
WeatherTech SportsCar Championship
Acura Motorsports competed in two class divisions last season – GT Daytona (GTD) and Prototype. Meyer Shank Racing earned two GTD course victories in the #86 NSX GT3 (at Belle Isle and Laguna Seca) and ended the season ranked 2nd overall. Acura Team Penske took the checkered flag in the ARX-05 Prototype at Mid-Ohio and finished the season in the 5th place spot. Acura also contended for both the Prototype and GTD manufacturers’ crowns in the Tequila Patron North American Endurance Championship, finishing second in Prototype and third in GTD.
 
While outstanding performances abounded in 2018, Katherine Legge was the clear standout. As lead driver of the #86 NSX GT3, she piloted the car to a second place overall team finish. She also contended for individual points leader, finishing just four points shy of first place. Her 329 overall points bested 105 other drivers.
PIKES PEAK
Acura Motorsports impressed off the track as well, setting three course records at the 2018 Pikes Peak International Hill Climb, a.k.a “Race to the Clouds.“ NSX earned a new hybrid record; TLX A-Spec set a new FWD record; TLX GT won the Open Class with a record-setting run by longtime Acura driver Peter Cunningham; and the 2019 Acura RDX also scored a podium finish in its race debut.
And speaking of Peter Cunningham, the legendary 56-year old racer also piloted the TLX GT to victory at the Empire Hill Climb in Michigan, besting a 40-car field while winning new Acura fans along the way. To quote Autoweek:
“Make no mistake about it: Cunningham and the Acura was a rock star at Empire. The driver and the RealTime Racing team on-site took it all in stride, from the extra fans crowding the start line when the Acura lined up to the well-wishers that came up to him during lunch hour at Joe’s Friendly Tavern.“
